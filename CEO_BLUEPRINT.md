# 4. THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE

Creator agencies face a structural operations problem that grows with every creator they add.

## The Core Problems

### 1. Content Volume Without Infrastructure
A 10-creator agency needs to coordinate 50–200 pieces of content per month across multiple platforms, formats, and brand partners. There is no system. Everything is manual.

### 2. Brand Deal Pipeline Is a Mess
Deals move through stages — outreach, negotiation, contract, live, payment — and tracking this for 10+ creators simultaneously is a full-time job done badly in a spreadsheet.

### 3. Reporting Takes Hours Every Week
Agencies need to report to creators, to brands, and internally. This is done manually, inconsistently, and late — eroding trust on all sides.

### 4. AI Tools Exist But No One Integrates Them
ChatGPT, Notion AI, CapCut, Descript — agencies know these tools exist. They don't have the time or expertise to build a working system from them.

### 5. Hiring Is Not the Answer
A content manager costs £28,000–£45,000/year plus oncosts. They get sick, leave, and need managing. The economics of hiring to solve an operations problem are broken below a certain scale.

## The Result

Agencies hit an operational ceiling. They stop taking on new creators not because demand is low but because operations can't keep up. They plateau. We remove the ceiling.

# 5. OUR SOLUTION & SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

## What We Build

For each client, we build a **bespoke AI content operations system** that covers:

1. **Creator Content Engine** — AI-generated content for each creator, personalised by niche, voice, and platform. Captions, scripts, hooks, repurposing suggestions.

# 9. TECHNOLOGY STACK

## Infrastructure

| Component | Tool | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Frontend (app) | React + Vite | Free |
| Frontend (website) | React + Vite | Free |
| Hosting (both) | Vercel (free tier) | £0 |
| Database + Auth | Supabase (free tier) | £0 |
| Email delivery | Resend (free tier) | £0 |
| Domain | Squarespace DNS | ~£12/yr |
| AI content | OpenAI API | Pay per use |

**Current monthly infrastructure cost: £0–£50** (depending on OpenAI API usage)

## Key Technical Decisions

**No sidebar navigation:** The portal uses a full-width, tab-based layout — premium feel, mobile-friendly, no visual clutter.

**RLS (Row Level Security):** Every database table is protected at the database level. Clients cannot see each other's data. Admin access gated via `is_admin()` security definer function.

**Content syncs to Supabase:** Generated content is written to the `content_queue` table. Clients see it in their portal without any localStorage dependency. Admin and client are fully decoupled.

**Service role for Edge Functions:** The weekly report Edge Function uses a `SVC_ROLE_KEY` secret to bypass RLS and read all client data. This is intentional — Edge Functions are server-side only.

**Automated email via pg_cron:** A Supabase cron job (`0 8 * * 1`) calls the Edge Function every Monday at 8am UTC. No manual trigger required.

## Database Schema

```
profiles         — user accounts, role (admin/client), is_active, onboarded
creators         — creator name, niche, platform, audience, linked to client
deals            — brand deal pipeline, stage, value, linked to creator
content_queue    — generated content pieces, status, linked to creator
```

# 15. CLIENT SUCCESS & RETENTION

## Why Clients Stay

The primary driver of churn in a managed service is: *"I don't feel like I'm getting value."*

We prevent this by making value visible, automated, and consistent.

**What keeps clients paying:**
1. Weekly automated reports (they see output every Monday without asking)
2. Live portal (they can check content and deals at any time)
3. Measurable output (content pieces generated, deals tracked)
4. Low friction (they don't need to manage us; we manage ourselves)

## Churn Warning Signs

- Client hasn't logged into the portal in 3+ weeks
- Client is slow to respond to check-in messages
- Client questions pricing without prompting
- Client mentions they've been "reviewing costs"

**Response:** Proactively reach out, offer a brief call, demonstrate new output, and if necessary offer a temporary discount rather than losing the retainer entirely.

## Retention Tactics

- **Monthly check-in call** (Growth and Agency tiers): 20 minutes, show output, discuss next month
- **Quarterly system review:** Identify new capabilities to add to their system
- **Expansion conversations:** At 3 months, ask about adding more creators (natural upsell to higher tier)
- **Case study request at 6 months:** Clients who agree to a case study are significantly less likely to churn — they're invested in the relationship

# 17. LEGAL, IP & CONTRACTUAL STRUCTURE

## Company Structure

Cornerstone AI Group should operate as a **UK Limited Company** (Ltd). This:
- Limits personal liability
- Enables VAT registration at appropriate threshold
- Provides professional credibility with clients
- Enables future investment or acquisition if desired

**Action required:** Incorporate via Companies House (£50, same day online).

## Intellectual Property

**We own the system. Permanently. Without exception.**

Every client contract must make clear:
- The AI system, workflows, prompts, and infrastructure built for the client remain CAIG's intellectual property
- The client is purchasing **access** to a managed service, not a deliverable or owned product
- Access is contingent on active retainer payments
- Upon termination, access is revoked with 30 days notice (client-initiated) or immediately (non-payment)

**This is the core structural protection of the business model.**

## Contract Structure

Every client must sign a **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** before setup begins, covering:
- Scope of service
- IP ownership (as above)
- Payment terms (setup upfront, monthly retainer by standing order or direct debit)
- Termination conditions (30-day notice client-side; immediate on non-payment)
- Confidentiality
- Data processing (GDPR compliance, what data we process and how)
- Limitation of liability

**Note:** Have an MSA drafted by a UK commercial solicitor. Cost: £500–£1,500. This is a necessary investment before the first client signs.

## GDPR Considerations

We process client data (creator profiles, deal information). As data processor:
- Have a Privacy Policy on the website (link in footer — required by law)
- Have a Terms of Service / Client Agreement
- Maintain a basic Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)
- Ensure Supabase data is processed in an appropriate region (EU or UK)

# 19. LONG-TERM VISION: THE CAIG GROUP

## The 5-Year Vision

Cornerstone AI Group is the first brand within a broader group of AI-powered managed service businesses. The long-term model mirrors a professional services group — multiple specialised divisions under one holding company, each serving a distinct niche with managed AI operations.

## CAIG Group Structure (Target: Year 3–5)

```
CAIG GROUP (Holding)
├── Cornerstone AI Group — Creator Agencies (current)
├── [Division 2] — E-commerce Brands (AI content + CX ops)
├── [Division 3] — B2B Companies (AI sales ops + outreach)
└── [Division 4] — Real Estate / Property (AI marketing ops)
```

Each division follows the same model:
- Build the system for an internal use case first
- Productise and sell managed access as a retainer
- Enter a market where operations is the bottleneck, not the product
- Own the IP; never sell it

## Why This Model Scales

- Each division uses the same infrastructure (Supabase, Vercel, Resend)
- Core systems are reusable across divisions with niche customisation
- The management layer (CEO, finance, admin) is shared
- Revenue is highly predictable (retainers across multiple divisions)
- The business can be valued as a recurring revenue business for acquisition or investment

## The Acquisition Thesis

At £500,000–£1,000,000 ARR across divisions, CAIG Group is an attractive acquisition target for:
- AI services holding companies
- Private equity firms targeting the creator economy or professional services
- Large agencies looking to bring AI ops in-house

At 3–5x ARR multiple (conservative for SaaS-like recurring revenue), this represents a **£1.5M–£5M exit** within 5 years from a standing start.

# APPENDIX B: GLOSSARY

**MRR** — Monthly Recurring Revenue. The total value of active monthly retainers.
**ARR** — Annual Recurring Revenue. MRR × 12.
**LTV** — Lifetime Value. Total expected revenue from a client over their relationship with CAIG.
